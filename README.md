## English Version

**LLM‑SLWI (Large Language Models for Second-Language Writing Interventions)**

The LLM‑SLWI project develops a rigorous, multi‑level empirical framework for the causal evaluation of large language models (LLMs) as second‑language writing interventions. Triangulating fine‑grained process traces (e.g. keystroke and interaction logs), writing product quality, learner strategy use and metacognitive regulation, and equity‑sensitive outcome measures, the framework models how learners write with LLM support, what kinds of texts they produce, how their strategies evolve over time, and which learners benefit under which instructional conditions. This design enables identification of causal effects and heterogeneous treatment impacts of LLM‑mediated feedback across proficiency levels, task types and sociolinguistic groups, and supports systematic characterisation of failure modes and unintended consequences in authentic classroom settings. Ultimately, LLM‑SLWI aims to derive evidence‑based design principles and guardrails for the safe, pedagogically robust and scalable deployment of large language models as socio‑technical partners in second‑language writing development.


## 中文版本

**LLM‑SLWI（大型语言模型在第二语言写作干预中的应用）**

LLM‑SLWI 项目致力于构建一个严谨的多层次实证框架，用于因果评估大型语言模型（LLM）作为第二语言写作干预工具的效果。该框架综合整合精细化的写作过程轨迹数据（如键击与人机交互日志）、写作产出质量指标、学习者策略运用与元认知调控行为，以及关注公平性的结果指标，从而建模学习者在 LLM 支持下 “如何写作” “写出何种文本” “如何随时间调整策略”，以及 “哪些学习者在何种教学条件下获益”。在此基础上，项目能够识别 LLM 介入写作反馈的因果效应及其在不同语言水平、任务类型和社会语言群体中的异质性影响，并系统刻画其在真实课堂情境中的失效模式与意外后果。最终，LLM‑SLWI 旨在提出基于证据的设计原则与安全边界，为大型语言模型作为第二语言写作发展中的社会技术伙伴，实现安全、教学有效且可规模化的应用提供坚实的理论与实证支撑。
